Lefty

The whole game is played using a controller with all the controls mapped to the left side of the controller, forcing the player to adapt to succeed by playing with only the left hand.

The game is also designed by switching around common concepts of sidescrollers: Lefty can move from left to right, but levels are designed with the most challenging parts going from right to left, while going from left to right are reserved for more calm moments. Each level is also oriented backwards from common platformers, Lefty always begins a level on the right side and must navigate it to find the exit on the left side. The overall goal is to disorient the player and make him feel vulnerable while experiencing what being a newly forced left-handed person feels like.

Lastly, "Lefty" is inspired by my childhood love of the 16-bit era. The game's aesthetic is curated to emulate the look and feel of a long lost Sega Genesis game or at least a distorted memory of what it was like playing on a CRT television screen.

Corporate Ladder Inc.

Corporate Ladder Inc. is a light-hearted puzzle platformer created for the Winter Game Jam 2025 at Lasalle College. It tied for first-place for best game made by students. The whole game was created in just under 5 weeks.

I've had the privilege of being the Lead Game Designer and the Lead Narrative Designer for this project. Early on we've established the general concept and decided to use humor to convey the tone of the whole game.

For this project, I've established the general concept along with the Lead Level Designer, from then we've established the whole flow of the game along with the different mechanics encountered throughout the levels.

Explorers of the Weird Unknown

This procedural Tabletop RPG casts players into the roles of dimensionauts tasked with exploring alternate realities. Four archetypes are available to balance the gameplay; the Soldier, the Scientist, the Engineer and the Jack of All Trades.

The rules are lite, simple to learn in a few minutes and a suite of tools are provided for the Game Master to generate countless worlds and challenges on the fly for the players to explore.

High stakes, fast action and loads of fun at the table is in store in the first game I ever had the pleasure of designing!

Boomer Fragfest

This project was a simple exercise in my Game Design class where our teacher sent us a project to use with game variables and settings all wrong.

We've had to figure out on our own what to change and adjust to make the game "feel good". As a fan of Old School FPS of the 90's, I went for a Quake vibe.

I've also decided to integrate my game and my level design skills for this demo, using the environment and audio voice over to guide the player in the environment as well as giving subtle cues of what dangers lie in the world.
